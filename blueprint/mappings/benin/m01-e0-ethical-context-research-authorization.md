## 1. Decision requested

The program board is asked to authorize only the bounded, minimal-risk methods in this dossier after every release condition in §2.2 is evidenced. Approval would permit staff-led M01 context research with competent adults, using voluntary interviews and authorized non-intrusive site observations, solely to shape the curriculum deployment profile. It would not authorize:

- research with anyone under 18;
- clinical, biomedical, epidemiological, traditional-medicine, or health-systems research;
- collection of health, biometric, genetic, political, religious, ethnic-origin, trade-union, sex-life, sexual-orientation, precise-location, government-identifier, financial-account, or other sensitive/high-risk data;
- covert observation, deception, scraping of personal profiles, or recording without separate express permission;
- medical, employment, admissions, grading, disciplinary, or benefit decisions;
- student-led field research or the operational M05 entry diagnostic;
- data transfer outside Benin or use of an externally hosted processor unless the data-protection route is approved and documented;
- public release of raw notes, quotations attributable by identity or context, faces, voices, signatures, contact details, partner-confidential information, or site-security details.

Anything outside this envelope requires an amendment, fresh risk and legal review, any applicable APDP/CNERS/institutional/partner approval, updated participant information, and re-consent where required.

## 3. Preliminary named-jurisdiction screen

### 3.1 Classification rule

The program applies the stricter of applicable law, regulator direction, institutional policy, partner conditions, funder conditions, and this protocol. The ethical reviewer records one of four dispositions for every method:

- **A — authorized:** all prerequisites evidenced; use exactly as approved;
- **C — authorized with conditions:** named controls must be satisfied and checked before each use;
- **H — hold:** applicability or authority unresolved; no recruitment or collection;
- **P — prohibited by this protocol:** not permitted without a formally approved amendment.

| Risk | Plausible pathway | Baseline control | Stop/response threshold | Residual risk after control |
|---|---|---|---|---|

| Financial inducement | payment is large relative to time/cost or withheld after withdrawal | pre-approved reimbursement/acknowledgement; disclosed before consent; not answer- or completion-dependent | participant cites payment as inability to refuse; unapproved payment request | low–moderate |

## 5. Scope-determination workflow

Before approving a method, the research lead and independent ethical reviewer answer in order:

1. **Purpose:** Is the activity designed to produce knowledge about people, their experience, behavior, needs, work, or community? If yes, treat it as human-participant research under program policy.
2. **Health boundary:** Does it seek health knowledge, patient/user health information, epidemiological inference, care-delivery performance, traditional-medicine knowledge, or health-system management evidence? If yes or unclear, stop and obtain a CNERS/local legal scope determination.
3. **Population:** Can every participant independently consent and confirm age 18+? If no, the baseline protocol does not apply.
4. **Data:** Can the objective be met without identity, contact details in the research dataset, sensitive data, precise location, recording, or confidential business information? If yes, remove those fields. If no, amend and review.
5. **Relationship:** Can a recruiter, teacher, manager, clinician, public official, aid provider, or partner affect the participant? If yes, separate recruitment/consent and add anti-coercion controls; hold if independence cannot be achieved.
6. **Place and safety:** Is a competent authority able to grant access and control hazards? If no, do not enter or observe.
7. **Processing:** Are controller, processors, locations, security, retention, rights, and APDP route approved? If no, do not collect.
8. **Output:** Can the planned reporting avoid re-identification, confidential disclosure, stereotyping, and unsupported claims? If no, redesign.

The signed determination records rationale and evidence; a project title or the word “educational” never settles applicability.

## 6. Approved-purpose specification

### 6.1 Purpose

Collect evidence necessary to instantiate a dated Benin deployment profile for the universal Electronics Systems Engineering curriculum, specifically:

- learner access/readiness and educator/laboratory conditions at aggregate level;
- professional electronics tasks, instruments, workflows, failure modes, and graduate skill needs;
- community and user constraints that can become measurable engineering requirements;
- site infrastructure, repair, supply, fabrication, test, service, and maintenance capability;
- constraints on language, accessibility, power, connectivity, cost, travel, scheduling, and participation;
- candidate context briefs and partner engagement boundaries.

### 6.2 Prohibited or incompatible purposes

M01 data may not be used to rank or diagnose an identifiable person, determine admission or employment, grade learning, manage performance, advertise partner endorsement, investigate misconduct, profile political/social identity, market a product, train an AI model, or determine eligibility for a service. It may not be combined with M05 data or institutional records without a new approved purpose and route.

### 6.3 Output contract

Authorized public outputs are aggregate findings, non-identifying personas explicitly marked as composites, sourced context briefs, and non-identifying capability constraints. Every claim records source class, collection period, sample limit, owner, reviewer, and next review. Quotations are paraphrased by default. Exact quotations require specific approval and a documented re-identification assessment.

## 7. Sampling and recruitment specification

### 7.1 Proposed initial envelope

These are maximum planning ranges, not quotas or claims of statistical representation. The approved protocol instance sets exact limits.

| Group | Proposed range | Coverage logic | Baseline exclusions |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Adult prospective/current learners | 24–36 individual interviews | variation in academic-language route, prior study, gender, geography/commute, work/care responsibilities, device/connectivity access, and requested access format | under 18; researcher's own assessed learners unless independently recruited and consented |
| Educators, laboratory staff, technicians, accessibility/support staff | 12–20 | subject, role, institution type, laboratory access, language, and experience | direct supervisor present during consent/interview |
| Electronics employers, manufacturers, repairers, public/community organizations, startups, researchers | 24–40 | sector and value-chain coverage; large/small/formal/informal organizations; urban/non-urban reach | questions about patients/customers or identifiable employees; unapproved confidential topics |
| Authorized site observations | 8–15 | teaching labs, repair, fabrication/assembly/test, field-service, and infrastructure contrasts | private homes; clinical care areas; active hazardous work outside host-controlled boundary |
| Instrument-pilot participants | 6–10 across two rounds | EN/FR, oral/read routes, accessibility, learner/partner roles | pilot content is not retained as substantive research evidence |

### 7.2 Sampling standard

- Use purposive maximum-variation sampling tied to a documented coverage matrix, then limited snowball referral only where it does not amplify one network.
- Report recruitment channels, invitations, acceptances, withdrawals, missing groups, and reasons known without collecting names in the analysis file.
- Do not claim national prevalence, statistical representativeness, cultural traits, or sector completeness from this sample.
- Stop when coverage is adequate and additional interviews produce no material requirement class, while remaining within the approved maximum. “Saturation” must be stated by topic and group, not as a universal fact.
- Community gatekeeper permission never replaces individual consent; refusal by a person is final even if a leader or employer agreed.

### 7.3 Recruitment safeguards

Recruitment uses a standard invitation stating purpose, duration, voluntary nature, privacy limits, compensation, eligibility, and independent contact. No repeated pressure: one invitation and one reminder at least seven days later, unless the person initiates contact. Teachers and managers may distribute a neutral invitation but receive no list of responders. Recruiters record only channel, broad group, date, response state, and follow-up permission.

## 8. Consent, withdrawal, and participant rights

### 8.1 Valid consent process

Consent is a conversation supported by the approved [EN information/consent form](../../../curriculum/templates/context-research/participant-information-and-consent-en.md) or [FR information/consent form](../../../curriculum/templates/context-research/participant-information-and-consent-fr.md), not a signature alone.

The researcher must:

1. provide the information in the participant's chosen available language and format before data collection;
2. separate consent to participate, audio, photography/video, exact quotation, future contact, and future reuse—unchecked means no;
3. explain that the institution, teacher, employer, public body, aid provider, or partner will not receive the person's participation choice or identifiable answers;
4. use a teach-back prompt: “In your own words, what will happen, what is optional, and how can you stop?”;
5. correct misunderstandings without coaching agreement, allow questions, and obtain explicit consent;
6. give the participant a copy or durable oral/contact route;
7. record form version, date, researcher, route, and consent choices separately from research notes.

Oral consent is allowed only where the ethics/data route approves it and a witness or auditable record does not create more risk. The reason must be recorded without stigmatizing the participant.

### 8.2 Withdrawal

Participants may skip any question, pause, or stop without giving a reason or losing the approved reimbursement/acknowledgement. They may request deletion of identifiable raw data until the stated **withdrawal cutoff**, proposed as 30 calendar days after their session or before irreversible aggregation/anonymization, whichever occurs first. The final approved form must state the exact cutoff and explain that truly anonymized aggregate data cannot later be isolated.

Requests go to the controller contact, not necessarily the interviewer. Identity verification must be proportionate; do not collect a new identity document merely to process a request. The custodian logs receipt, scope, verification, action, datasets/backups affected, completion date, and response without retaining unnecessary request content.

### 8.3 Limits of confidentiality

The form must state locally reviewed limits, such as a credible immediate threat or a legally mandatory disclosure. Researchers must not improvise promises of absolute confidentiality. If an unexpected safeguarding or legal issue arises, pause, collect no more detail than needed for immediate safety, and contact the designated ethical/safeguarding authority.

## 9. Privacy and data-minimization specification

### 9.1 Data architecture

Use three separated stores:

| Store | Contents | Access | Public repository? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Contact/consent store | contact details, signed or witnessed consent, withdrawal key, payment/reimbursement evidence | custodian and specifically authorized compliance staff | never |
| Research store | participant ID, approved non-sensitive fields, notes/transcript, analysis codes | named research team members according to role | never raw; only approved aggregate export |
| Publication store | aggregates, de-identified constraints, approved context briefs, source metadata | release-reviewed program team/public as designated | yes, after disclosure review |

The linkage key is stored separately from both contact and research data. IDs encode no site, role, gender, language, or other attribute. Paper forms use sealed transfer and locked storage; researchers do not retain personal copies.

### 9.2 Baseline data dictionary

Collect only:

- random participant ID and broad participant group;
- broad location at the lowest resolution necessary for the engineering constraint;
- role/experience in bands where relevant, avoiding unique job titles;
- chosen interview and communication route;
- non-diagnostic access needs stated as preferred formats/supports;
- responses to approved curriculum, infrastructure, workflow, skill, maintenance, cost-band, language, access, and engineering-constraint questions;
- consent choices, session date/duration, instrument version, interviewer/interpreter ID;
- non-identifying field context and researcher reflexive notes.

Do not copy national identifiers, exact birth dates, identity documents, student/employee numbers, personal addresses, bank/mobile-money details into research data, health/disability diagnoses, precise religion/ethnicity/politics, immigration status, union status, sexual life/orientation, biometrics, exact device identifiers, passwords, confidential customer/patient records, or allegations about named persons.

### 9.3 Proposed retention schedule

The controller and APDP/institutional route must approve or replace these periods before use:

| Record | Proposed maximum | Disposal |
|---|---:|---|
| Unselected recruitment contacts | 30 days after recruitment closes | secure deletion/shredding |
| Contact details for a completed session | withdrawal cutoff plus 30 days, unless future contact is separately opted in | secure deletion; retain only non-identifying status |
| Audio | until verified transcript, normally 14 days and never more than 30 days | cryptographic/managed-device deletion and deletion log |
| Raw identifiable/pseudonymous notes and transcript | 12 months after M01 report acceptance | secure deletion or irreversible anonymization |
| De-identified analytic dataset and claim trail | 24 months after deployment-profile release | delete or approve a justified archival extension |
| Consent, approval, incident, access, deletion, and release audit records | 5 years after protocol closure or the approved institutional/legal period | confidential records disposition |
| Partner-confidential material | shortest contractually and legally approved period; never by default beyond analysis need | return or secure deletion with partner record |

Backups follow the same expiry, with documented deletion at the next controlled backup cycle. A legal hold pauses deletion only through named authority and is recorded.

### 9.4 Security minimum

- institution-managed, encrypted devices and restricted storage; unique accounts and least privilege;
- multi-factor authentication where available; offline-safe credentials and recovery route;
- no data on personal email, consumer messaging, removable media, or unapproved survey/transcription/AI services;
- encryption in transit and at rest; paper locked and logged;
- access review before collection, monthly during active work, and at staff departure;
- daily field transfer where safe, verified backup, and deletion from collection device after transfer verification;
- incident reporting immediately and no later than the internally approved maximum; the data lead determines regulator/participant notification with qualified advice;
- tested restore and tested secure deletion before release.

## 10. Accessibility, language, interpreter, and compensation controls

### 10.1 Accessibility and language

EN and FR forms are coequal controlled versions. The participant chooses the available language and format. Offer, where feasible, accessible digital, large-print, screen-reader-ready, low-literacy oral, quiet/private, captioned/transcribed, extra-time, and physically accessible routes. Ask “What format or support would help you take part?” rather than requesting a diagnosis.

Community/user languages may be added after translation, back-check or independent review, interpreter briefing, and pilot. A local-language route supplements rather than replaces EN/FR academic parity. Record only the route used, not an inference about identity or nationality.

### 10.2 Interpreter protocol

The interpreter signs confidentiality and conflict declarations, translates meaning without summarizing, uses first person, marks uncertainty, and does not answer for the participant. Avoid a supervisor, teacher, family member, community gatekeeper, or child as interpreter. The participant may reject an interpreter. The researcher reconfirms consent and sensitive boundaries after interpretation. Interpreter identity is recorded by staff ID, not in the research response.

### 10.3 Compensation/benefit protocol

The protocol instance records currency (XOF), amount, basis, payer, payment route, tax/finance treatment, and whether it is travel reimbursement, connectivity reimbursement, refreshment, time acknowledgement, or partner in-kind support. It must be proportionate to actual cost/time, equal for equivalent participation, and approved before invitation. Participants hear both the amount and the fact that payment does not depend on answers or completion.

Do not promise jobs, admission, grades, procurement, product delivery, funding, preferred partner status, medical benefit, or community infrastructure. Return an accessible summary of findings and disposition of contributed ideas; this is knowledge return, not a guaranteed direct benefit.

## 11. Site, travel, and recording authorization

Every visit uses the [site-visit risk and authorization record](../../../curriculum/templates/context-research/site-visit-risk-and-authorization.md).

### 11.1 Minimum site controls

- written permission from a representative with actual authority for the site and topic;
- a named host, sign-in/out, permitted zones, escort rule, access hours, emergency contacts, evacuation point, first-aid route, communications/check-in, and transport plan;
- hazard screen for electrical/stored energy, machinery, traffic, work at height, heat/weather, chemicals/dust/fumes, biological exposure, fire, security/conflict, animals, water, fatigue, lone work, and accessibility;
- required PPE and competence, prohibited actions, equipment isolation boundary, and photography/recording decision;
- participant privacy: notice to affected workers, a genuine non-participation route, no observation in toilets/changing/rest/private care areas, and no visible records/screens;
- no touching, operating, sampling, disassembly, measurement, or connection to equipment unless separately risk-assessed and technically authorized;
- daily field brief and dynamic reassessment when conditions change.

### 11.2 Recording hierarchy

1. structured non-identifying notes;
2. researcher-created diagram with confidential details removed;
3. non-identifying equipment image only if site owner approves and no person/personal/confidential/security information can enter the frame;
4. audio only on approved necessity and separate participant choice;
5. identifiable photo/video is outside the baseline.

Researchers show no live participant material through presentations, personal devices, or social media. Partner permission does not replace each person's permission.

## 12. Incident, stop-work, and restart procedure

### 12.1 Anyone may stop work

Any participant, researcher, interpreter, worker, host, partner, or reviewer may say “stop,” “pause,” or the agreed local-language equivalent. The team immediately stops questioning/recording, makes the situation safe, and checks what the person wants. No retaliation, grading, employment, payment, or partnership consequence follows a good-faith stop.

### 12.2 Mandatory stop triggers

- consent is absent, misunderstood, withdrawn, or contradicted by behavior;
- a minor or person unable to consent independently is encountered;
- a teacher/manager/gatekeeper pressures or monitors participation;
- an unapproved sensitive, health, confidential, or identifying data stream appears;
- an unconsented person, screen, record, voice, image, or restricted process is captured;
- physical conditions differ from the approved assessment, required PPE/escort is absent, or the host calls an emergency/evacuation;
- harassment, discrimination, conflict, distress, illness, fatigue, security threat, transport failure, or loss of communication makes continuation unsafe;
- device/paper loss, unauthorized access, malware, misdirected message, or any suspected confidentiality breach occurs;
- a serious adverse allegation or immediate safeguarding concern arises;
- method, site, population, processor, purpose, or compensation differs materially from approval.

### 12.3 Response sequence

1. stop collection and recording; protect life and immediate safety first;
2. do not delete, alter, promise secrecy, confront, or investigate beyond what safety requires;
3. contact the site emergency authority for physical danger and the named institutional incident/ethics/data contact as applicable;
4. preserve minimal evidence securely, isolate affected device/material, and record time, place, people notified, and action without unnecessary personal detail;
5. inform affected participants through the controller's approved route where required;
6. ethical/data/safety authorities classify impact, reporting duties, corrective action, data disposition, and participant remedy;
7. pause all similar methods if the failure may be systemic.

### 12.4 Restart authority

Only the authority controlling the stopped facet may restart it in writing: the site/field-safety authority for physical risk, controller/data authority for processing, independent ethics reviewer for consent/safeguarding, and partner acceptance authority for access/confidentiality. Material amendments return to the original approving bodies. Schedule pressure is never restart evidence.

## 14. Instrument pilot and acceptance test

Use the [instrument pilot record](../../../curriculum/templates/context-research/instrument-pilot-record.md). Pilot data tests the process and is not included as substantive M01 evidence.

### 14.1 Two-round design

- **Round 1, cognitive test:** 3–5 adults across intended roles and EN/FR/read/oral routes; think-aloud and teach-back on purpose, voluntariness, optional recording, privacy limits, withdrawal, compensation, and contacts.
- **Round 2, field rehearsal:** 3–5 different adults; complete recruitment, consent, interview/observation simulation, secure transfer, withdrawal request, incident drill, and deletion test. Include at least one intended accessibility route and an interpreter route if either will be offered.

Pilot participants receive the approved pilot compensation and privacy notice. Their answers to substantive questions are destroyed after defect analysis unless a separate approved protocol authorizes reuse.

### 14.2 Acceptance thresholds

- 100% can state participation is optional, questions may be skipped, and stopping has no penalty;
- 100% distinguish participation from recording/publication and identify the chosen recording state;
- at least 90% accurately explain purpose, main data use, confidentiality limit, withdrawal route/cutoff, and independent contact without leading; any miss on a material point triggers revision and retest;
- zero observed authority pressure, stigmatizing assumption, hidden mandatory field, collection outside the dictionary, or unapproved data transfer;
- median completion time is within the advertised duration and no pilot exceeds it by more than 25% without a participant-controlled break or adjusted notice;
- EN/FR technical meaning and choices match; any additional language interpretation passes independent meaning check;
- every offered route is usable with keyboard/screen reader/large print/oral delivery as applicable, and the venue/process supports privacy and safe access;
- secure transfer, access restriction, withdrawal lookup, deletion, backup disposition, incident contact, and stop/restart drill all pass;
- every defect has severity, owner, correction, retest evidence, and closure decision.

A critical consent, coercion, safety, confidentiality, or accessibility defect resets the affected pilot round. The ethical reviewer, data custodian, accessibility reviewer, EN/FR reviewers, and research lead sign acceptance within their competence.
