# M05 — Entry Diagnostic and Bridge Program

**Goal:** identify readiness without exclusion and provide a 4–6 week practical route into mathematics, technical language, computing, safety, and measurement.

**Indicative duration:** 8–12 weeks to create and pilot  
**Primary owners:** first-year lead, mathematics/physics/communication faculty, lab instructor  
**Depends on:** M01 and M04  
**Authoring entry:** preliminary M01 learner evidence plus the reviewed M04 unit design/manifest pattern are available for reversible drafting\
**Release gate:** M04 exemplar findings and applicable M01 language/access evidence are dispositioned\
**Exit unlocks:** equitable S1 entry

## Epic E1 — Diagnostic specification

- [ ] **M05-E1-T01 [SPEC]** Define constructs and non-punitive use for algebra, trigonometry, graphs, proportional reasoning, SI units, vectors, physical intuition, reading, computing, and tool recognition.
- [ ] **M05-E1-T02 [MAT]** Author equivalent EN/FR items, short practical stations, scoring guides, and learner-friendly results.
- [ ] **M05-E1-T03 [SPEC]** Map every diagnostic pattern to targeted bridge activity, tutoring, accessibility support, or direct entry.
- [ ] **M05-E1-T04 [TEST]** Pilot item clarity, timing, language choice, device access, and practical station logistics.
- [ ] **M05-E1-T05 [REV]** Moderate for construct validity and ensure the test does not confuse English/French fluency with engineering readiness.
- [ ] **M05-E1-T06 [TEST]** Check routing decision consistency, plausible false-negative/false-positive cases, accessible construct-equivalent stations, subgroup/language patterns, and whether every recommended support route actually has capacity.

## Epic E2 — Hands-on bridge units

- [ ] **M05-E2-T01 [MAT]** Unit 1: quantities, estimation, SI units, scientific notation, significant figures, calculator/spreadsheet use, dimensional checks, and measuring everyday low-voltage sources/components.
- [ ] **M05-E2-T02 [MAT]** Unit 2: algebra, logarithm intuition, and graphs through resistor/sensor data; students plot, fit, interpolate, transform, and challenge a model.
- [ ] **M05-E2-T03 [MAT]** Unit 3: trigonometry, vectors, and complex-number intuition through forces, rotations, waves, and phasor preview using physical demonstrations.
- [ ] **M05-E2-T04 [MAT]** Unit 4: files, terminal, editor, Python, plots, Git concepts, and offline documentation using a real dataset and repeatable workflow.
- [ ] **M05-E2-T05 [MAT]** Unit 5: breadboard topology, continuity, polarity, source/load, current limiting, DMM, and fault isolation.
- [ ] **M05-E2-T06 [MAT]** Unit 6: bilingual technical reading, glossary, datasheet structure, notebook evidence, retrieval/spaced practice, productive help-seeking, and five-minute explanation.
- [ ] **M05-E2-T07 [MAT]** Thread the engineering-agency routine through every unit: estimate → predict → build/inspect → measure → compare → debug → document → defend, using locally and internationally engineered examples without tokenism.
- [ ] **M05-E2-T08 [SPEC]** Apply L0/L1 supervision and energization rules; bridge completion never grants unsupervised bench authorization outside the recorded competence decision.

## Epic E3 — Support and mastery route

- [ ] **M05-E3-T01 [SPEC]** Create flexible schedules, printed/offline packs, loan equipment, peer tutoring, and instructor consultation.
- [ ] **M05-E3-T02 [MAT]** Provide retrieval practice, worked examples, multiple representations, and extension route for advanced entrants.
- [ ] **M05-E3-T03 [TEST]** Use short reassessments and an integrated practical rather than attendance to establish readiness.
- [ ] **M05-E3-T04 [SPEC]** Define handoff notes to S1 instructors while protecting student privacy and avoiding deficit labels.
- [ ] **M05-E3-T05 [TEST]** Measure bridge workload, completion, confidence, and subsequent S1 performance under approved consent/legal basis, data minimization, retention, privacy, and cohort-level reporting; revise.

## Epic E4 — Correct, approve, and release

- [ ] **M05-E4-T01 [REV]** Analyze technical, diagnostic-decision, language, access, workload, support-capacity, practical, and teaching defects.
- [ ] **M05-E4-T02 [MAT]** Correct EN/FR diagnostic forms, bridge units, instructor pack, remediation links, and offline/print assets together.
- [ ] **M05-E4-T03 [TEST]** Re-pilot changed routing-critical, safety-critical, accessibility-critical, or practical work and verify the support route exists.
- [ ] **M05-E4-T04 [REL]** Independent authority approves and tags the diagnostic/bridge release with QA record, known limitations, retention rules, and S1 handoff.

## Hands-on and real-life safeguard

At least half the bridge meetings use measured objects, physical circuits, datasets, or tool practice. Mathematics is never presented as a detached prerequisite: every topic feeds a visible engineering question. The exit task is to build, measure, plot, diagnose, and explain a current-limited sensor/divider—not a multiple-choice exam alone.

## Required deliverables

- Diagnostic construct, forms/variants, decision rules, scoring, validation, and learner-facing result pack
- Six-unit EN/FR bridge, instructor notes, integrated practical, support/remediation operations plan, and accessible alternatives
- Pilot/validation evidence, privacy/retention record, QA/defect disposition, offline archive, and tagged release

## Exit criteria

- [ ] Diagnostic reliably routes learners to specific support.
- [ ] Bridge materials exist in EN/FR and offline/printable forms.
- [ ] Safety/tool exit practical aligns with the start of ESE101/ESE111.
- [ ] Learners can bypass mastered work without losing orientation.
- [ ] No diagnostic decision routes a learner to unavailable support or excludes them from the program.
- [ ] Independent release approval records zero open critical safety, validity, access, or language defects.
