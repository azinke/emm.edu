# M06 — Semester 1 Content Production

**Goal:** create the complete coordinated S1 package for ESE101, MAT101, PHY101, CSC101, ESE111, COM101, program competence gates G1 and G2, and Student Project M1.

**Indicative duration:** 5–7 months  
**Primary owners:** S1 course team and integration lead  
**Depends on:** M04–M05; M03 first-year inventory ready  
**Authoring entry:** reviewed M04 vertical-unit technical contract, M05 diagnostic/bridge interface contract, and M03 safe first-year bench baseline are approved for reversible drafting\
**Release gate:** M04/M05 pilot findings and M03 first-year quantities, staff pilot, spares, and fallbacks are dispositioned\
**Exit unlocks:** M07 and first S1 delivery

## Hands-on and real-life safeguard

Each teaching week includes an observable artifact or decision: instrument setting, wired circuit, measured dataset, tested program, fault hypothesis, figure, or design choice. M1 must compare predictions against a changing physical source/load and produce a bounded claim; a dashboard screenshot alone is not evidence.

## Required deliverables

- Approved S1 integration contract, workload map, six complete manifests/packages, and unit-level claim/QA records
- Prepared lab/fixture/inventory pack, G1/G2 specifications and examiner/remediation pack
- Project M1 brief, golden/fault artifacts, V0 contribution dossier, verification and individual evidence pack
- Staff/second-instructor pilot evidence and signed pilot-ready EN/FR/offline cohort release

## Exit criteria

- [ ] All six course packages satisfy the resource manifest and EN/FR parity gate.
- [ ] Every physical activity was piloted and has realistic ranges/fallbacks.
- [ ] Workload fits 30 ECTS and dependencies align week by week.
- [ ] G1/G2 and M1 distinguish individual competence from team output.
- [ ] Every learner-facing unit is pilot-ready; G1/G2 are always recorded per student rather than inferred from a team or sample.
