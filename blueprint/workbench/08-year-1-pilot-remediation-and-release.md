# M08 — Year-1 Pilot, Remediation, and Release

**Goal:** teach Year 1 to a limited cohort, collect direct learning/operations evidence, correct the materials, and publish a dependable bilingual release.

**Indicative duration:** two delivery waves (S1 then S2) plus a 6–8 week correction window; calendar dates follow the actual institution\
**Primary owners:** Year-1 lead, course owners, assessment coordinator, lab/translation/accessibility leads  
**Depends on:** M06–M07  
**Authoring entry:** M06 and M07 pilot-ready cohort baselines pass their release gates\
**Release gate:** both delivery waves, correction/re-pilot, external review, and stable-release approval are complete\
**Exit unlocks:** stable prerequisites for Years 2–4

## Epic E1 — Pilot readiness

- [ ] **M08-E1-T01 [REV]** Audit every weekly asset, instructor assignment, room, kit, spare, safety authorization, assessment, and fallback before enrollment.
- [ ] **M08-E1-T02 [TEST]** Run faculty microteaching, lab dry-runs, assessor/rubric calibration, offline-build and local-server load tests.
- [ ] **M08-E1-T03 [SPEC]** Establish weekly defect triage, rapid safety correction, student support, equipment incident, and parity issue routes.
- [ ] **M08-E1-T04 [SPEC]** Freeze cohort versions and protect restricted solutions/assessments.
- [ ] **M08-E1-T05 [DEC]** Hold a formal go/no-go before each semester wave; before S2, verify each learner's actual S1 prerequisite and G1/G2 evidence or approved remediation route. Define representative-cohort rationale, consent/legal basis for program evaluation, privacy/retention, accessible feedback, critical-defect classes, residual-risk authority, and support period.

## Epic E2 — Evidence-rich pilot delivery

- [ ] **M08-E2-T01 [TEST]** Track actual time on chapters, psets, labs, projects, translation use, and remediation against ECTS estimates.
- [ ] **M08-E2-T02 [TEST]** Observe a sample of classes/labs for prediction, student action, measurement quality, role equity, debugging method, and instructor talk time.
- [ ] **M08-E2-T03 [TEST]** Collect direct evidence for PLO introductions, G1–G4, M1/M2, practicals, notebooks, code, and individual defenses.
- [ ] **M08-E2-T04 [TEST]** Monitor safety, near misses, failed components/equipment, access barriers, language challenge, attendance, and team-role distribution.
- [ ] **M08-E2-T05 [TEST]** Hold short student/instructor retrospectives while memories and faulty setups remain available.
- [ ] **M08-E2-T06 [TEST]** Track instructor/technician preparation and support load, equipment downtime/repair/replacement, per-student instrument access, engineering-agency/belonging indicators, and cross-language/equipment-path standard equivalence.

## Epic E3 — Analyze and remediate

- [ ] **M08-E3-T01 [REV]** Separate technical misconception, prerequisite gap, wording, workload, apparatus, assessment, and teaching-sequence causes.
- [ ] **M08-E3-T02 [TEST]** Compare EN/FR item and completion evidence cautiously; review possible differential item functioning.
- [ ] **M08-E3-T03 [MAT]** Offer targeted remediation and reassessment for unmet gates; verify transfer with changed values/setups.
- [ ] **M08-E3-T04 [SPEC]** Prioritize defects by safety, technical correctness, dependency risk, equity, and frequency.
- [ ] **M08-E3-T05 [REV]** Review whether real-life briefs actually changed decisions and whether labs became recipes in delivery.
- [ ] **M08-E3-T06 [REV]** Review each G1–G4 decision for variant equivalence, assessor agreement, mastery threshold, language/access fairness, and successful transfer on reassessment; every student has a pass or an active remediation/reassessment record.

## Epic E4 — Correct and release

- [ ] **M08-E4-T01 [MAT]** Revise both languages, professor notes, timing, expected ranges, hints, assessments, and inventory from evidence.
- [ ] **M08-E4-T02 [TEST]** Re-pilot changed safety-critical, concept-critical, or hardware-dependent activities.
- [ ] **M08-E4-T03 [REV]** Conduct conflict-declared external review covering Year-1 theory/practice, bilingual pedagogy, deployment context, international-level expectations, workload, and representative raw evidence; disposition every finding.
- [ ] **M08-E4-T04 [REL]** Tag Year-1 stable release with change log, QA records, known limitations, offline archive, and migration note.
- [ ] **M08-E4-T05 [SPEC]** Feed prerequisite and production lessons into M09–M17.

## Hands-on and real-life safeguard

The pilot observation rubric must verify that students, not instructors, handle instruments, construct/test code and circuits, choose diagnostic actions, and explain evidence. Any unit that technically “covered” content but lacked student action is reopened.

## Required deliverables

- S1/S2 go/no-go records, frozen cohort manifests, pilot/evaluation protocol, consent/privacy/retention and support plans
- Direct outcome/G1–G4 evidence dataset, workload/access/operations report, defect and decision log, remediation/reassessment record
- External-review evidence and disposition, corrected QA records, stable EN/FR/offline release archive, migration/support note

## Exit criteria

- [ ] Actual workload is credible for 60 ECTS.
- [ ] G1–G4 decisions are reliable, remediable, and language-equitable.
- [ ] Project M1/Project M2 and labs have physical evidence, and every learner has the required individual competence record; sampling is used only to moderate the evidence system.
- [ ] All critical defects are closed; lower defects are visible and owned.
- [ ] Stable EN/FR Year-1 release builds offline and can be taught by prepared staff.
- [ ] Release authority accepts zero open critical defects and only time-bounded, impact-assessed residual defects.
- [ ] M09 receives a signed findings-disposition record; Year-1 pilot completion is a release gate, not a hard barrier to earlier reversible S3 drafting.
