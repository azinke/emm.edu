# M15 — Semester 7 Specialization and Capstone Launch

**Goal:** create RES401, ESE401, ENT401, an approved elective portfolio, CAP401, and M7 so students can define and retire risk in a defensible advanced product.

**Indicative duration:** 9–12 months; sponsor/project recruitment begins earlier  
**Primary owners:** final-year lead, research/security/enterprise faculty, elective leads, capstone board  
**Depends on:** M13; incorporate M14 readiness evidence  
**Authoring entry:** M13 capstone interface, the M00 radar mandate/source register, and the candidate-project proposal/screening contract with initial candidate sources are approved\
**Release gate:** applicable M14 readiness findings and one-term-early course/elective/capstone workflow pilots are dispositioned\
**Exit unlocks:** M16

## Hands-on and real-life safeguard

Capstone proposals begin with observed need and acceptance evidence, not preferred technology. Each team must physically or experimentally retire its top technical risks in S7. A literature review, CAD rendering, or AI-generated architecture without measured prototype evidence cannot pass CDR.

## Required deliverables

- Initial radar snapshot and approved coherent elective portfolio with complete manifests, staff/learner pilots, capacity/equipment/cost paths
- Complete RES401/ESE401/ENT401 paired packages, claim registers, QA and one-term-early staff evidence
- Capstone pipeline/handbook, entry decisions, staff-dry-run evidence, approved proposals and V3/V4 contribution plans
- Tagged controlled Project M7 baselines and signed pilot-ready S7 EN/FR/offline release

## Exit criteria

- [ ] Offered electives have complete, piloted resources and real benchmark/design evidence.
- [ ] Every capstone has approved need, scope, safety/data/IP, requirements, architecture, risks, verification, budget, and owner.
- [ ] M7 demonstrates the riskiest interfaces/phenomena rather than only easy features.
- [ ] CAP402 can proceed without unresolved fatal procurement or authorization dependency.
- [ ] Every CAP401 entrant has a recorded prerequisite/gate/M6/proposal clearance; team admission never supplies missing individual evidence.
