# M16 — Semester 8 Capstone, Internship, and Handover

**Goal:** create and operate CAP402, IND402, ELE4C, SEM402, program competence gate G9, and Student Project M8 so each team releases and hands over a reproducible system while every graduate independently verifies and defends attributable work.

**Indicative duration:** 9–12 months including placement coordination  
**Primary owners:** capstone director, internship/clinic coordinator, seminar/elective leads, external panel  
**Depends on:** M15  
**Authoring entry:** M15 CAP402 interfaces, placement/clinic contracts, and final-elective contract are approved\
**Release gate:** M15 findings and one-term-early CAP402, elective, G9, reproduction, and handover pilots are dispositioned\
**Exit unlocks:** M17

## Epic E1 — S8 teaching and placement packages

- [ ] **M16-E1-T01 [MAT]** Produce CAP402 guidance for implementation, configuration, TRR, qualification, field acceptance, manufacturing/service, defense, release, and retrospective.
- [ ] **M16-E1-T02 [MAT]** Produce IND402 objectives, workplace safety, supervisor agreement, professional log, evidence/confidentiality route, feedback, ethics, and reflective technical report.
- [ ] **M16-E1-T03 [MAT]** Produce SEM402 horizon-scanning, standards watch, research replication, technology readiness, societal analysis, and bilingual briefing package.
- [ ] **M16-E1-T04 [REV]** Select and revalidate the already produced/piloted M15 ELE4C package for the actual cohort: confirm depth-cluster continuity, capacity, apparatus/cost, claim currency, capstone interface, EN/FR/accessibility parity, and no material change requiring re-pilot.
- [ ] **M16-E1-T05 [SPEC]** Provide applied engineering clinic route when equitable placements are unavailable; require equivalent supervised technical contribution.
- [ ] **M16-E1-T06 [DEC]** Clear each CAP402 team only after passed CDR plus approved verification, safety/security, ethics/data, procurement, local-value, and acceptance plans; record individual CAP402 eligibility.
- [ ] **M16-E1-T07 [SPEC]** Complete the named CAP402, IND402, and SEM402 course/unit manifests and vertical child records, including IND402 supervisor rubric/evidence/confidentiality artifacts and SEM402 professional-pathways plus reproduced-benchmark evidence; link the revalidated ELE4C manifest rather than duplicating it.

## Epic E2 — Implement and verify M8

- [ ] **M16-E2-T01 [HW]** Implement controlled hardware, firmware, HDL, enclosure, fixtures, and manufacturing artifacts required by the approved CDR baseline; no project adds a domain merely to satisfy a list.
- [ ] **M16-E2-T02 [TEST]** Perform unpowered inspection, current-limited bring-up, subsystem tests, integration boundaries, stress/fault/recovery, long-duration, environmental, security, and field/user tests as applicable.
- [ ] **M16-E2-T03 [TEST]** Trace every requirement to procedure, raw evidence, result, deviation, and acceptance; investigate anomalies rather than delete them.
- [ ] **M16-E2-T04 [SPEC]** Maintain change control, approved BOM alternates, SBOM, hardware BOM/HBOM, calibration, credential/key lifecycle, risk, incident, limitation, and decision records.
- [ ] **M16-E2-T05 [REV]** Conduct TRR, qualification, manufacturing/service, acceptance, and release reviews.
- [ ] **M16-E2-T06 [REL]** Tag a controlled Project M8 release candidate—with checksums, exact configuration, deviations, limitations, and reproduction instructions—before the independent reproduction and handover tests.

## Epic E3 — Reproducibility and handover

- [ ] **M16-E3-T01 [TEST]** Have an independent team reproduce all software/HDL builds and a clean build of a critical hardware subsystem—or the full system where feasible—plus its key measurement from tagged source; bound every reproducibility claim to what was actually rebuilt.
- [ ] **M16-E3-T02 [TEST]** Have a representative user/technician operate the system and locate/repair a safe seeded service fault using the preferred-language/pictorial guide; parity-test the separate EN/FR technical editions.
- [ ] **M16-E3-T03 [MAT]** Deliver bilingual user, installation, calibration, service, update/recovery, safety, and retirement documentation appropriate to the audience.
- [ ] **M16-E3-T04 [SPEC]** Transfer ownership, spares, credentials/keys with secure rotation/destruction record, data, maintenance schedule, training, warranties/limitations, and decommission/return/disposal route.
- [ ] **M16-E3-T05 [DEC]** Obtain acceptance, limited acceptance with deviations, or documented removal/closure.
- [ ] **M16-E3-T06 [TEST]** Execute the approved V3/V4 local build/test/service plan or record why scope prevents it; maintain contribution/provenance, sample-sized pilot-batch first-pass/defect/rework evidence where applicable, supplier/capability gaps, and truthful origin wording without unsupported yield claims.
- [ ] **M16-E3-T07 [REL]** After the acceptance/limited-acceptance decision and completed transfer, tag the final accepted product configuration and dossier; if the result is removed or closed instead, archive a non-release closure tag that cannot be mistaken for an accepted product.

## Epic E4 — Individual G9 and graduate defense

- [ ] **M16-E4-T01 [TEST]** Conduct individual oral/practical defense with unseen parameter change or fault.
- [ ] **M16-E4-T02 [TEST]** Ask each student to connect stakeholder need, model, architecture, implementation, test evidence, uncertainty, safety/security, cost, and lifecycle.
- [ ] **M16-E4-T03 [REV]** External panel calibrates scoring and samples repositories/raw evidence, not only presentations.
- [ ] **M16-E4-T04 [MAT]** Remediate and reassess specific unmet competencies without giving team credit as individual evidence.
- [ ] **M16-E4-T05 [REL]** Archive releasable portfolio, restricted evidence, panel decision, and lessons learned.
- [ ] **M16-E4-T06 [REV]** Conduct the required Project M8/team and individual retrospective: original assumptions, failures, changes, evidence quality, stakeholder/product impact, local capability gained, unresolved risk, and next owner/action.
- [ ] **M16-E4-T07 [TEST]** Staff dry-run G9 variants, independent reproduction, handover, credential transfer, accessibility, and panel calibration at least one term before use.

## Hands-on and real-life safeguard

The final demonstration is only one test point. Qualification must include boundary, failure, recovery, duration, user, service, and field evidence appropriate to the claims. Edge AI is accepted only against a simple baseline with field-held-out behavior, resource/energy cost, false alarms, shift, and fallback.

## Required deliverables

- Complete CAP402/IND402/ELE4C/SEM402 manifests, paired resources, staff/learner pilots, placement/clinic contracts, and CAP402 entry records
- Controlled Project M8 implementation, verification/qualification evidence, SBOM/HBOM and V3/V4 contribution dossier
- Independent bounded reproduction, preferred-language user/service test, secure ownership/credential/data transfer, acceptance/closure record
- Individual G9 decisions/remediation, external panel record, retrospective, tagged product and teaching-material archives

## Exit criteria

- [ ] Every critical safety, security, regulatory, or ethical requirement has accepted evidence; an unmet one blocks operation, acceptance, and product release. Other unmet requirements require approved scope reduction, explicitly limited acceptance, or removal—an undocumented or merely listed deviation never passes.
- [ ] Another person can reproduce the independently tested build scope and can operate, diagnose, maintain, and decommission the result within the explicitly bounded claim.
- [ ] Every candidate cleared for graduation independently passes G9 and can defend limitations honestly; any non-passer has a recorded noncompletion/remediation hold rather than team-derived credit.
- [ ] Placement/clinic and seminar outcomes have direct evidence.
- [ ] Partner/data/IP obligations are closed or transferred in writing.
