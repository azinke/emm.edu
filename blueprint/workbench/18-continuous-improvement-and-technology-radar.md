# M18 — Continuous Improvement and Technology Radar

**Goal:** keep the released program safe, current, locally relevant, bilingual, accessible, and reproducible without destabilizing active cohorts.

**Cadence:** course review every offering; semester review; annual release; architecture review every three years  
**Primary owners:** program board and named course/platform/language owners  
**Depends on:** M17; repeats indefinitely

## Epic E1 — Evidence loop every offering

- [ ] **M18-E1-T01 [TEST]** Collect outcome, program-gate, assessment-item, workload, lab, equipment, incident, access, language, project, partner, graduate/employer/alumni, technician, supplier, manufacturing, repair, and service evidence.
- [ ] **M18-E1-T02 [REV]** Hold course retrospectives with students, instructors, technicians, and relevant partners.
- [ ] **M18-E1-T03 [SPEC]** Convert each improvement into observation → interpretation → action → owner/date → later effectiveness check.
- [ ] **M18-E1-T04 [MAT]** Correct urgent technical, safety, or security issues in both languages immediately under change control, learner-impact review, notification, and reassessment decision where applicable.
- [ ] **M18-E1-T05 [TEST]** Re-pilot changed safety-, security-, hardware-, assessment-, prerequisite-, accessibility-, or language-equity-critical work.

## Epic E2 — Annual technology and standards radar

- [ ] **M18-E2-T01 [SPEC]** Inherit and maintain the M00/M15 radar baseline; review semiconductors, RISC-V, FPGA/open EDA, RTOS/Linux, IC/open PDK, power/storage, communications, edge AI, security, sensors, manufacture, standards, and education evidence.
- [ ] **M18-E2-T02 [DEC]** Classify candidates Watch, Trial, Adopt, Core, or Retire using learning value, maturity, documentation, offline tools, the named deployment profile's place/currency/cost/supply evidence, safety, faculty, lifecycle, and ethics.
- [ ] **M18-E2-T03 [TEST]** Require a reproduced benchmark or learning pilot before adoption; compare with current/simple baseline.
- [ ] **M18-E2-T04 [SPEC]** Review current deployment-jurisdiction radio, data, safety, product/origin, environmental, accreditation, and import rules with authoritative source, effective/retrieval date, reviewer/owner, uncertainty, and next review.
- [ ] **M18-E2-T05 [DEC]** Limit routine annual churn; keep active cohort tool/hardware freezes unless safety/security demands change.

## Epic E3 — Platform, supply, and security maintenance

- [ ] **M18-E3-T01 [SPEC]** Requote deployment-currency landed cost—XOF for Benin—and source currency with rate/date, tax/duty/freight, quote source, availability, lifecycle, counterfeit risk, substitutes, spares, calibration, and repair data.
- [ ] **M18-E3-T02 [TEST]** Run golden projects on primary/substitute boards and supported operating systems/toolchains.
- [ ] **M18-E3-T03 [TEST]** Review dependency vulnerabilities, supported RTOS/Linux/tool versions, archived installers/docs, and update/migration paths.
- [ ] **M18-E3-T04 [DEC]** Retire unsafe/unmaintained platforms with a tested migration, adapter/API path, inventory disposition, and content updates.
- [ ] **M18-E3-T05 [TEST]** Restore the repository and offline bundle from backup annually.

## Epic E4 — Content, language, and assessment health

- [ ] **M18-E4-T01 [REV]** Review technical errata, citations/links, termbase, translation parity, accessible figures/media, and professor-note usefulness.
- [ ] **M18-E4-T02 [TEST]** Analyze MCQ/practical/pset evidence, wording challenges, rubric calibration, and compromised items; test language/access differential item functioning only when samples support it and otherwise use cautious qualitative review.
- [ ] **M18-E4-T03 [MAT]** Refresh contexts using new field evidence while preserving varied international/scientific/industrial examples.
- [ ] **M18-E4-T04 [MAT]** Replace recipe-like labs with prediction, measurement, anomaly, debugging, and design where delivery evidence shows passivity.
- [ ] **M18-E4-T05 [REV]** Confirm AI/tool policy still rewards source checking, raw evidence, verification, and individual competence.

## Epic E5 — Annual release and longer review

- [ ] **M18-E5-T01 [REL]** Publish annual compatible release with migration note, known limitations, checksums, offline archive, and cohort support dates.
- [ ] **M18-E5-T02 [SPEC]** Maintain older cohort security/safety corrections through the declared support window.
- [ ] **M18-E5-T03 [REV]** Run external course samples annually and a full program architecture review every three years.
- [ ] **M18-E5-T04 [DEC]** Revisit PEOs and the full deployment/accreditation mapping on relevant regulator/accreditor change and at least every five years.
- [ ] **M18-E5-T05 [SPEC]** Feed approved major changes into a separately planned next major release; never silently rewrite active outcomes.

## Required deliverables for each annual cycle

- Offering/semester evidence set, retrospectives, prior-action effectiveness report, and new owned improvement register
- Technology/standards and deployment-profile update with rule/source dates, platform/supply/security tests, cost/capability changes, and decisions
- Corrected paired content/assessment/accessibility records with targeted re-pilot evidence
- Annual compatible release, migration/limitations/support note, checksums, offline archive, and next-major backlog

## Hands-on and real-life safeguard

The annual report includes hands-on health indicators: percentage of core units with student-generated physical/data evidence, instrument access disaggregated by language/equipment path and relevant access factors, completed debug practicals, staff-piloted labs, project field/service evidence, student agency observations, and units reopened for passive delivery. It also samples whether contexts changed requirements, whether deployed systems remained useful/maintainable, and whether local design, assembly, test, calibration, repair, service, supplier and product-authority capability advanced at the claimed V0–V4 level.

## Exit criteria for each annual cycle

- [ ] Safety/security corrections and critical technical defects are closed in both languages.
- [ ] Supported platforms/toolchains pass golden tests and have supply/migration evidence.
- [ ] Prior-cycle assessment, workload, access, language, outcome, and local-capability actions have effectiveness evidence; every new action has an owner/date and a future check rather than a fabricated immediate effect.
- [ ] Annual release is reproducible offline and active cohorts remain supported.
- [ ] Technology changes reflect evidence and learning value, not novelty alone.
