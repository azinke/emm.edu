# Engagement acceptance criteria

Engagement means observable, inclusive engineering thinking—not entertainment,
noise, motion, attendance, or satisfaction alone. These criteria apply to sessions,
chapters with embedded activities, labs, studios, demonstrations, and pilots.

## Required session evidence

A normal interactive session passes only when all of the following are designed and
observed, unless an approved applicability decision explains a different format:

1. **Inclusive response route:** every learner can respond privately or publicly
   through an accessible method; a few volunteers do not represent the class.
2. **Retrieve or predict:** learners activate a prerequisite or commit to an
   expected direction/magnitude/behavior before the answer or result is shown.
3. **Reason:** learners explain, complete/fade a worked step, interpret data, rank
   hypotheses, or compare alternatives rather than only recall a label.
4. **Act on evidence:** learners calculate, manipulate a safe setup, inspect a
   primary source, run/modify code, measure, annotate, or analyze authentic data.
5. **Make a decision:** at least one diagnostic/design/model choice changes or is
   confirmed because of evidence; the acceptance criterion is explicit.
6. **Exit check:** individual evidence near the end reveals the next teaching
   action—advance, clarify, remediate, or enrich.

Purposeful reasoning events are spaced through the lesson, not compressed into one
closing quiz. Required hands-on units also preserve each learner's personal
construction, instrumentation, debugging, configuration, or defense evidence.

## Acceptance indicators

- A meaningful proportion is not defined by one universal number; the author sets
  a justified target for the mode/class and reports denominator, missingness, and
  access route. Every learner must have a feasible response route.
- Sample reasoning shows movement or productive conflict in the targeted model,
  not merely correct clicks after explanation.
- Learners use evidence to support, revise, or limit at least one claim/decision.
- The activity fits the time budget without sacrificing safe setup, individual
  evidence, interpretation, or exit check.
- Both language pathways and declared accessibility/equipment routes show equivalent
  cognitive and evidence opportunity; differences trigger investigation.
- Instructor records a specific next action linked to exit patterns.

## Rejection patterns

Return the unit for revision when learners only listen/copy/follow steps; prediction
comes after reveal; spectacle replaces interpretation; group output hides individual
reasoning; “Any questions?” is the participation route; speed or fluent language
is mistaken for competence; context is decorative; the activity has no decision;
enjoyment is the sole measure; or exit data are collected but never acted upon.

Safety overrides participation targets. An activity is stopped when required; the
pilot then records the stop, recovery, evidence impact, and re-pilot condition.
