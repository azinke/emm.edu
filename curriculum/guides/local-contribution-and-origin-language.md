# Local contribution, product authority, and origin-language guide

Describe verified work and authority precisely. Local value is not erased by
imported silicon or specialist fabrication, and local final assembly alone does not
prove local design ownership or lawful product origin. This guide does not provide
a jurisdictional origin ruling; regulated wording requires current qualified legal
review recorded in the contribution dossier.

## Evidence levels

| Level | Defensible statement pattern | Evidence that must exist |
|---|---|---|
| V0 — Inspect, maintain, learn | “[Named team] inspected, measured, diagnosed, repaired, and documented [configuration] at [location/date].” | Safe measurement/diagnosis record, repair and verification, limitations. |
| V1 — Adapt and integrate | “[Named team] translated documented user/context requirements into [specific interface/configuration/substitution] and verified the adaptation.” | Approved need/requirements, make/buy, interface/substitute source, acceptance evidence and user/technical review. |
| V2 — Design and release | “[Named team] controlled and released the [electronics/PCB/firmware/enclosure/test] design for version X; imported parts/services are listed.” | Original controlled design/configuration, decisions, verification, reproducible release package, licenses/dependencies. |
| V3 — Pilot-production readiness | “[Organization/team] executed a controlled repeat build/test of n units of version X at [location]; observed sample results were ….” | Traveler, serials, incoming inspection, checked fixture, actual n, first-pass count/fraction, defects/rework/scrap/cost, technician handoff. No generalized yield without justified larger sample. |
| V4 — Product authority | “[Authorized organization] controls requirements, configuration, supplier/product decisions, compliance, field quality, service, improvement, and end of life for version X within the stated scope.” | Accepted product dossier, decision rights, supplier/capability plan, compliance scope, field/quality/service evidence, training and lifecycle controls. |

Claim only the highest fully evidenced level. V4 is an ambition, not a synonym for
a polished capstone. A technically excellent project can honestly stop at V1–V3.

## Write from a contribution ledger

Before drafting publicity or assessment language, complete the
[`contribution dossier`](../templates/teaching-resources/contribution-dossier.md).
Name, for the exact version and period:

- who/where defined the need and measurable requirements;
- architecture, electronics, PCB, firmware/HDL/software, mechanical, enclosure,
  harness, sourcing, fabrication, assembly, test/calibration, documentation,
  deployment, service, improvement, and retirement contributions;
- imported components, proprietary modules, external fabrication/cloud/tooling,
  licensed designs/code/data, and partner-confidential work;
- who owns/controls source/design files, configuration, release and product
  decisions, and who can reproduce/service the product;
- personally attributable learner decisions versus team, institution, supplier,
  partner, or wider-location capability.

## Preferred wording

Use specific verbs and bounded nouns:

- “designed and verified the two-layer PCB for revision B”;
- “assembled and production-tested a five-unit pilot batch in Porto-Novo using
  imported semiconductors and locally fabricated enclosure/harness work”;
- “adapted the service interface from an approved user-language study”;
- “uses PCB fabrication by [supplier/location]; schematic, layout, firmware,
  assembly, test, and service configuration are controlled by [organization]”;
- “observed four first-pass units out of five in this batch; no generalized yield
  or process-capability claim is made.”

State product/version, actors, location of actual work, imported/external inputs,
sample and period, evidence boundary, and limitations.

## Wording that requires revision

- “100% local,” “fully indigenous,” or “locally manufactured” when any material
  dependency/contribution boundary is undisclosed;
- “Made in [place]” based only on team nationality, project location, final
  assembly, branding, or aspiration;
- “we designed it” when the team integrated an opaque reference design without
  control of architecture/files;
- “local capacity exists” based on one learner, one prototype, one partner, or one
  short pilot;
- “production-ready,” “field-proven,” “certified,” “reliable,” or “X% yield” when
  the evidence supports only a one-off build, limited sample, teaching test, or
  compliance overview;
- nation/continent-wide attribution for work performed by a named team/site.

## Product-origin decision

An origin statement is separate from the educational V0–V4 judgment. Before using
regulated “Made in …” or equivalent language, record exact proposed wording,
product/configuration and tariff/legal category where relevant, jurisdictions and
markets, transformation/value/process facts, qualified reviewer and authority,
law/guidance version/effective date, decision, limitations, and review triggers.
Where this decision is absent, publish contribution facts without an origin label.

## Review and correction

Reopen the dossier and all public wording when configuration, supplier, process,
location, contributor/decision rights, sample evidence, service ownership, or law
changes. Correct EN/FR statements simultaneously and notify affected project,
assessment, partner, release, and publicity owners. Aspiration may be described as
a future target only when clearly separated from current provenance.
