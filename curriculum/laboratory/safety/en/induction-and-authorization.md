# Safety induction and authorization pack

This public pack prepares learners and calibrates assessors. It does not confer G1,
G3 or an authorization. The safety authority creates unseen but equivalent live
variants in the restricted store and records results with `ESE-SAF-FRM-002`.

## Induction outcomes

The learner can locate local emergency controls; classify L0–L4 scope; explain why
voltage alone is insufficient; calculate fault power and capacitor energy; inspect
PPE, leads, fuses, probes and sources; perform the pre-energization sequence; use
stop-work authority; isolate and verify zero energy; and report an event/quarantine
without concealing evidence.

## Formative quiz

Choose the safest next action and justify it in one sentence.

1. A 9 V supply can deliver 20 A into a short. Is voltage alone enough to call it L1?
   A. yes; B. no, classify fault current/power and protection; C. yes with glasses;
   D. yes if measurement is brief.
2. A `4700 µF` capacitor is charged to `24 V`. Its stored energy is closest to:
   A. `0.14 J`; B. `0.68 J`; C. `1.35 J`; D. `2.71 J`.
3. A circuit draws twice the predicted current without visible damage. First action:
   A. raise the limit; B. touch parts for heat; C. isolate and diagnose; D. replace fuse.
4. An earth-referenced oscilloscope ground is to be clipped to an unverified converter
   switching node. Best action: A. proceed quickly; B. remove earth; C. stop and use
   the approved differential/isolation method under the required level; D. use thinner lead.
5. A lithium pack is swollen on return. Best action: A. charge-test; B. puncture;
   C. follow the no-contact damaged-pack quarantine route; D. put it in ordinary waste.
6. Who may call stop work? A. instructor only; B. authorized learners only; C. anyone;
   D. safety authority only.
7. A pre-G1 learner wants to energize an L1 circuit. This is allowed only when:
   A. alone below 12 V; B. a peer agrees; C. at a direct-supervised instructor-controlled
   station where the instructor controls energization; D. never under any circumstances.
8. Solder practice before G3 may begin: A. independently after G1; B. at L2 after task
   briefing under direct supervision; C. only after graduation; D. at home.
9. A near miss caused no damage. It is: A. ignored; B. reported and reviewed;
   C. erased to protect grades; D. recorded only if repeated.
10. A person may challenge G1 through prior competence by: A. showing a certificate only;
    B. skipping evidence; C. passing a formally assessed equivalent unseen gate at the
    same threshold; D. signing their own authorization.
11. After switching off, adjustment may begin when: A. LEDs are dark; B. one minute passes;
    C. all sources are isolated, discharge time observed and zero energy verified;
    D. a peer touches it safely.
12. An unknown solvent arrives without an SDS. It is: A. L2 by small quantity;
    B. used near a window; C. quarantined pending identification/assessment; D. diluted.

**Formative key:** 1 B; 2 C (`0.5 × 0.0047 × 24² = 1.35 J`); 3 C; 4 C; 5 C;
6 C; 7 C; 8 B; 9 B; 10 C; 11 C; 12 C. Wrong answers route to the matching manual
section and a changed-value reassessment; memorizing this key does not pass a gate.

## G1 practical station circuit

The live assessment uses function-checked, low-energy equipment and changed values.
The candidate may choose EN or FR and use the approved glossary/access supports.

| Station | Observable action | Required evidence | Critical stop failure |
|---|---|---|---|
| A — room and stop | locate isolation, exit, response card and quarantine point; state two stop conditions | examiner checklist and oral reason | cannot identify isolation/response or continues after `STOP` |
| B — de-energized inspection | find seeded lead/fuse/polarity/rating defect; select PPE | defect, consequence and disposition | attempts energization with defect |
| C — energy calculation | calculate current/fault power and capacitor energy; assign highest level | work with units and limit comparison | uses voltage as sole classifier |
| D — source setup | with output off, set voltage/current limit and check meter input/fuse | witnessed correct configuration | shorts source, wrong current input, or bypasses protection |
| E — controlled energization | complete checkpoint, clear hands, energize, compare first reading, stop on seeded anomaly | prediction, observation and safe isolation | touches/rewires energized setup or raises unexplained limit |
| F — shutdown/report | isolate all sources, wait/discharge, verify zero, tag a simulated defect and give reporting route | zero-energy check and complete verbal handoff | touches before verification or conceals event |

**G1 decision rule:** all six critical-safety rows pass and at least 10 of 12
published observable criteria are demonstrated. No total score compensates for a
critical failure, dishonest evidence or failure to stop. Maximum ordinary attempts,
retention, appeal and expiry are filled by the institution before administration.

## G3 soldering/rework construct

After an L2 briefing, practice may start under direct supervision before G3. The
independent-progression gate uses an unseen equivalent artifact and checks: extraction
and stand setup; PPE/hygiene; temperature/tool selection; controlled tinning/joint;
through-hole removal without pad damage; one bounded SMD rework; polarity/orientation;
inspection against published criteria; continuity/short check; thermal shutdown;
cleanup and waste. Any burn-risk act, disabled extraction, uncontrolled hot tool,
energized rework or dishonest inspection is conjunctive failure.

## Assessor calibration protocol

1. Safety authority freezes construct, variants, criteria, prompts and local response data.
2. Two assessors dry-run every station, verify energy limits/function checks and time it.
3. Assessors independently score one pass, one borderline and one critical-fail anchor
   (recorded or simulated); reconcile every disagreement before candidates enter.
4. Standard prompts request prediction or reason without naming the missing action.
   Stop unsafe behavior immediately; a stopped attempt remains valid evidence.
5. Double-mark at least the locally approved sample across EN/FR and access routes.
   Review pass rate, criterion disagreement and wording challenges without lowering threshold.
6. Record variant custody, instrument IDs, assessor names, calibration date and defects.
   A changed safety construct or unresolved rater disagreement blocks administration.

## Remediation and reassessment

The assessor records each missed criterion, observed misconception and immediate
restriction. The learner completes the smallest matching route: manual retrieval;
guided energy calculation; de-energized inspection practice; instructor-modeled
energization; stop/report role-play; solder tool handling on a cold station; or an
accessible alternative preserving the decision construct. A different value, fault
or setup then tests transfer. Critical unsafe action suspends relevant energization
until the safety authority authorizes reassessment. Record outcome, new expiry and
any residual restriction; never overwrite the original attempt.
