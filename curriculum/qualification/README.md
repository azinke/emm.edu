# Author and reviewer qualification route

**Qualification ID:** QUAL-AUTHOR-VERTICAL-01
**Owners:** learning-content architect and faculty-development lead
**Validity:** two years or until a major workflow/tool/safety change; role- and
hazard-specific qualifications can expire sooner

An author/reviewer qualifies by completing and independently reviewing one
non-assessed bilingual vertical sample. Completion is workflow evidence, not
authorization for a hazard level, live assessment, participant research, or
final release approval.

## Entry and roles

The candidate has completed repository/offline-tool orientation, applicable
safety induction, bilingual/accessibility basics, originality/licensing, and
restricted-data handling. Assign a candidate, technical reviewer, apparatus or
laboratory reviewer, EN editor, FR editor, accessibility reviewer, and release
observer. The candidate cannot solely approve any facet.

## Required sample journey

1. Select a safe, low-voltage, non-assessed question that changes a calculation,
   setup, test, or decision; freeze IDs, outcomes, prerequisites, misconception,
   safety scope, evidence, and applicability dispositions.
2. Complete a claim/source register and independently recompute the model,
   values, units, expected range, and review triggers.
3. Author a natural EN/FR pair plus shared technical contract, accessible figure
   or data representation, prediction, action, anomaly/fault, design decision,
   acceptance test, professor cue, and exit check.
4. Build the pair offline and run all positive and seeded-negative checks.
5. Physically execute the declared safe apparatus path. Retain setup identity,
   dated raw readings, uncertainty/range comparison, anomaly disposition,
   cleanup, and a second-person observation. Prepared data cannot satisfy this.
6. Obtain separate technical, apparatus/safety, EN, FR, accessibility, license,
   and build dispositions. Pilot instructions with a second prepared person.
7. Correct at least one authentic defect, rerun affected checks, record before /
   after / rationale, and create a controlled qualification release record.
8. Teach back the workflow to a new participant: classify one change, locate one
   restricted-data trap, explain one parity defect, and facilitate one
   hypothesis-led diagnostic without giving away the decision.

## Pass standard and register

All mandatory evidence is attributable and reproducible; no critical defect or
expired waiver remains; every facet is approved by its owner; physical execution
and EN/FR usability are real rather than inferred from linting. Record candidate,
roles, sample/version/commit, dates, apparatus, build checksums, facet evidence,
defect corrected, teach-back observer, limitations, expiry, and signatures in the
controlled staff register. Personal records stay outside public Git; only blank
templates and a redacted aggregate status may be public.

## Train-the-trainer authorization

A trainer holds current candidate qualification, observes two complete
qualifications, co-facilitates one, calibrates three sample dispositions against
the faculty-development lead, demonstrates stop-work/escalation and construct-
preserving feedback, and passes an annual drift review. Authorization is revoked
or remediated after unsafe guidance, approval conflicts, repeated parity misses,
restricted-data exposure, or calibration drift.
