## 4. Approved interview modules

Select only modules needed by the protocol. The approved instance replaces bracketed text with exact paired prompts and response fields.

### A. Role and system boundary

- Ask for the participant's broad role and interaction with the relevant system; do not record a unique title if it would identify them.
- Establish which parts they directly know, which are estimates, and which they cannot discuss.
- Record experience only in approved broad bands where necessary.

### B. Observable workflow or learning condition

- Ask for a recent typical process from start to finish, including tools, inputs, outputs, handoffs, wait/rework, and evidence used to decide success.
- Ask what can be directly observed or measured. Separate first-hand observation from hearsay.
- For learner/access research, ask about available routes, formats, time, power/connectivity/device/tool access, cost bands, and support—not diagnosis, exact income, home address, or deficit labels.

### C. Failure, constraint, and consequence

- Ask what commonly prevents the process or learning route from working, how the issue is detected, and what happens next.
- Seek measurable ranges only where the participant is authorized and able to estimate them; record unit, range, uncertainty, and source class.
- Do not ask who caused a failure, request an allegation, or record a customer's/patient's/student's case.

### D. Current workaround, maintenance, and access

- Ask how people adapt, repair, substitute, communicate, or recover; record tools/capability and boundary, not a named person's performance.
- Ask what would make a proposed project maintainable and acceptable, who would own service/handover decisions, and what exit condition is necessary.
- Do not present an improvised workaround as approved safe practice.

### E. Curriculum implication and validation

- Ask what a graduate should be able to **demonstrate**, with what artifact, measurement, decision, or fault evidence.
- Ask which constraint would materially change a requirement, laboratory, project, support route, or partner agreement.
- Ask what evidence or second source could verify the statement and what might make it stale.

### F. Closing and participant control

- Summarize only the main non-sensitive constraints; ask what is inaccurate or missing without pressuring further disclosure.
- Ask whether any statement should be removed from the record before closeout.
- Re-state withdrawal cutoff/contact, compensation, output/return route, and no-promise boundary.
- Ask permission before any approved future contact; default is none.

## 6. Non-participant observation module

Use only with current site authorization and worker/participant notice. Observation means non-intervention unless the approved method explicitly says otherwise.

| Observation ID/time band | Approved area/process | Observable event/equipment/state | Measurement or non-identifying evidence | Expected/actual variation | No-person/no-record/privacy check | Hazard/change/stop state | Context needed to avoid overclaim |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
|  |  |  |  |  |  |  |  |

Do not record names, faces, voices, badges, handwriting, exact schedules, customer/patient/student activity, live screens, documents, serials, access controls, precise geolocation, or individual behavior/performance. Do not infer consent from presence. If a person enters the observation boundary and avoidance is not possible, stop or reposition.
