# Practical/design assessment [ID]

## Construct specification

Map outcomes/levels to observable actions and rubric rows. State authentic task,
prerequisite gates, time, independence/team boundary, language choices, allowed
resources, tool/AI policy, reassessment variant rule, and evidence retention.

## Candidate brief

Give goal/non-goals, context-changing constraints, input evidence, interface and
hazard boundaries, deliverables, checkpoints, acceptance tests, and what the
learner must predict, make/measure/debug/decide, and defend.

## Safety and access

State authorization, PPE/energy controls, mandatory inspection, stop conditions,
unsafe-action rule, accessible manipulation/communication route, and how assistance
preserves learner direction and individual reasoning.

## Analytic rubric and decision rule

Cover requirements, model/reasoning, implementation, measurement/test, debugging,
communication/configuration, and responsibility. Publish Level 3 as default
mastery unless approved otherwise; safety/integrity rows are conjunctive. Define
cut, required rows, reassessment, moderation, appeal, and no-compensation gates.

## Examiner calibration

Provide anchors, standardized prompts that do not coach the construct, timing,
evidence capture, inter-rater sample, language-equity guidance, anomaly response,
defective-task disposition, and pre-administration dry run.
