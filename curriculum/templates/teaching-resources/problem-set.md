# Problem set [ID]: [engineering theme]

## Purpose, outcomes, and prerequisite check

State workload, submission format, collaboration/AI/tool-use policy, staged-hint
policy, novelty versus worked examples, numerical-variant source, and rubric.

## Conventions and provided evidence

Define symbols, sign/reference convention, units, rounding, assumptions, data
provenance, source versions, and figure descriptions. EN/FR editions use equivalent
parameters and cognitive demand.

## A. Foundations and estimation (F)

Include retrieval and an order-of-magnitude bound before detailed calculation.

## B. Modeling and derivation (M)

Require model selection, assumptions, derivation, units, sanity check, and limits.

## C. Datasheet or standard navigation (D)

Require primary-source version/page/table/condition, not a copied isolated number.

## D. Simulation/code and interpretation (S)

Require reproducible inputs/tool version, checks, and interpretation beyond output.

## E. Experimental data and uncertainty (X)

Retain raw data; quantify uncertainty, residuals, anomalies, and expected range.

## F. Debug from evidence (B)

Provide symptoms and incomplete evidence. Ask for ranked hypotheses, the best next
discriminating test, expected outcomes, root cause, correction, and prevention.

## G. Open design under constraints (O)

Require a trade study, explicit requirement-changing context, acceptance test, and
defensible limitation.

## H. Context, ethics, and communication (R)

Require evidence-based reasoning about safety, security, accessibility,
sustainability, user impact, lifecycle, or contribution/origin as applicable.

## Submission and self-check

- [ ] Estimates, units, assumptions, and validity stated.
- [ ] Sources/data/code and tool use are attributable and reproducible.
- [ ] Negative/anomalous evidence is retained and investigated.
- [ ] Design claims do not exceed the required evidence-ladder rung.
- [ ] Figures are accessible and all required individual evidence is identifiable.
