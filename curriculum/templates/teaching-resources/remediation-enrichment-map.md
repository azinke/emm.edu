# [Course] remediation and enrichment map

## Routing principles

Routes respond to named evidence, preserve the same outcome/safety threshold, and
lead to a new check. They do not label learners permanently or turn optional
enrichment into an unstated prerequisite. Safety holds and gates follow their
controlled requalification rules.

## Unit outcome routes

For each outcome include:

- prerequisite retrieval and bridge;
- model/notation alternate explanation;
- guided worked example followed by faded support;
- safe apparatus/tool rehearsal where needed;
- misconception-specific evidence or seeded fault;
- reassessment variant that tests transfer, not memorized repetition;
- escalation for repeated difficulty or accessibility barriers.

## Enrichment routes

| Outcome already demonstrated | Extension | Higher reasoning/evidence rung | Boundary (not required for mastery) | Review/feedback |
|---|---|---|---|---|

Enrichment deepens model limits, transfer, independent reproduction, design
trade-offs, or local product authority; it is not merely more routine questions.

## Monitoring and revision

Track uptake, completion, recheck success, language/access patterns, time burden,
and recurring source defects. Revise the core unit when many learners need the
same remediation rather than normalizing the defect.
